About Me

Dost Muhammad is a dedicated AI researcher with a BS (Hons) in Computer Science and an MS in Artificial Intelligence. Currently pursuing a PhD at the School of Computer Science, University of Galway, Ireland (CRT-AI & ADAPT centres).

His work focuses on Explainable AI (XAI), eXplainable Deep Learning, Responsible AI, Multimodal AI, Knowledge Graphs, and Medical Imaging.

Research Focus

Exploring the frontiers of Explainable AI and its applications

Explainable AI (XAI)

Developing transparent and interpretable AI systems that provide insights into their decision-making processes.

eXplainable Deep Learning

Creating methods to interpret and explain complex deep learning models for various applications.

Evaluation Metrics for XAI

Designing quantitative and qualitative metrics to assess the effectiveness of XAI methods.

Multi-models & Knowledge Graphs

Integrating multiple AI models and leveraging knowledge graphs for enhanced reasoning and explainability.

Explainable NLP

Developing interpretable natural language processing models that provide transparent text analysis.

Applying AI and XAI techniques to medical imaging for improved diagnosis and treatment planning.

Research Approach

My research follows an interdisciplinary approach, combining theoretical foundations with practical applications. I focus on developing AI systems that are not only accurate but also transparent, interpretable, and trustworthy.

By bridging the gap between complex AI models and human understanding, my work aims to make AI more accessible and reliable for critical applications in healthcare, finance, and other domains where decision transparency is essential.
